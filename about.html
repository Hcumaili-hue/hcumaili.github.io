About the Project

A textual-historical exploration of religious development

This project is not a presentation of beliefs, nor a defense of inherited positions. It is an attempt to return to the texts themselves.

The study traces the development of religious language and structure from the earliest accessible layers of the Torah, through early Christian reinterpretations, and toward later textual continuity.

Rather than reading these traditions as fixed systems, the project approaches them as evolving textual formations — shaped by narrative tension, reinterpretation, and theological reconstruction.

Methodology

  • Textual Analysis: examining internal tensions and structural shifts
  • Historical Reading: placing texts within their developmental context
  • Conceptual Tracking: following key ideas across time

The project begins with the Torah, not as a closed canon, but as a starting point — a foundation upon which later readings are built.

Subsequent sections will explore early Christianity as a re-reading of this foundation, followed by a broader comparative analysis across traditions.

This is not a search for confirmation. It is an attempt to understand how the text itself speaks — and how meaning emerges through its transformations.
